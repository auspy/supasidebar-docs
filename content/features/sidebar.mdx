# Sidebar Panel

The SupaSidebar panel is a floating sidebar for macOS that provides instant access to your saved links, files, and folders from any application.

![Sidebar Panel position on macOS](/assets/default%20position%20of%20sidebar.jpeg)

## Features

- **Multi-Screen Support** - Automatically appears on your active display
- **Left or Right Positioning** - Toggle sides instantly with `Cmd Ctrl L`
- **Auto-hide** - Optionally hides when clicking outside the panel
- **Drag-to-Resize** - Drag the inner edge of the sidebar to adjust its width
- **Pin to Spaces** - Follow across all macOS desktop spaces or stay on one
- **Slide Animation** - Smoothly slides in and out when opening or closing. The animation direction is context-aware based on the sidebar's screen position
- **Noise Texture** - A subtle texture overlay for a more tactile feel (10% opacity by default, configurable in Appearance)
- **Custom Colors** - Pick any color for an individual link or folder so it stands out. Available from the right-click menu under **Color**, and applied in both the full sidebar and compact mode.

## Quick settings menu

The ••• menu in the sidebar top bar provides quick access to common sidebar settings without opening full Preferences. It also includes a **Keyboard Shortcuts** item that shows your configured shortcuts, and a **Quit** option to close SupaSidebar.

## Settings

Configure the sidebar panel in **Preferences**. Sidebar preferences use a split-panel layout with use case presets on the left and individual settings on the right. Settings have clear descriptions to help you understand what each option does.

### Appearance

Found in **Preferences - Appearance**.

- **Theme** - Choose System, Light, or Dark. System follows your macOS appearance. The choice applies across the sidebar, command panel, web preview, notifications, tooltips, and floating button. You can also switch it from the macOS menu bar under **SupaSidebar - Appearance**, or by typing "dark", "light", "theme", or "appearance" in the [Command Panel](/features/commandPanel).
- **Text size** - Slider that scales sidebar text from 85% to 150%. Applied live across the app, including the command panel and notes.
- **Width** - Drag the sidebar inner edge to resize
- **Opacity on focus change** - Dim sidebar when not focused
- **Glass theme** - Enable translucent background
- **Noise texture** - Adjust the overlay texture intensity (0-100%)
- **Show item counts** - Display count badges on folders
- **Arrow indicator** - Show expand/collapse arrows on folders

### Behavior

Found in **Preferences - General**.

- **Auto-hide on deactivate** - Hide sidebar when clicking outside
- **Default side** - Choose left or right screen edge
- **Auto show with browser** - Show sidebar when browser is active. See [Live Tabs](/features/liveTabs)
- **Adaptive height** - Adjust sidebar height based on content
- **Auto-Switch Side on Clipped Browser** - Flips the sidebar to the opposite side when your preferred side would be cut off by an adjacent monitor. Off by default.
- **Bring Browser to Front on Show** - When on, opening the sidebar also brings your browser forward. Turn off to keep your current app frontmost when the sidebar opens.
- **When Sidebar Opens** - Choose what happens to your browser window the moment the sidebar appears:
  - **Do Nothing** - leave the browser window exactly where it is.
  - **Prevent Overlap** - shift the browser sideways so the sidebar isn't covering it. See [Prevent Browser Overlap](/features/browserIntegration/smart).
  - **Fill Freed Space** - resize the browser to take up the screen minus the sidebar's column, so the two share the screen with no overlap. See [Fill Entire Screen](/features/browserIntegration/fillScreen). Works in both attached and independent sidebar layouts.
- **When Sidebar Hides** - Choose what happens to your browser window when the sidebar goes away:
  - **Expand to Fill** (default when "Fill Freed Space" or [Auto-Tile](/features/browserIntegration/tiling) is on) - browser windows grow to fill the full screen. Tiled windows re-tile across the entire screen width.
  - **Restore Browser** - browser windows return to their size and position from before the sidebar was opened, so an overlay panel doesn't permanently reshape your window.
  - **Leave It** - do nothing; the browser stays exactly where it was while the sidebar was open.

  > **Firefox and Zen users:** the resize options shape browser windows. Firefox and Zen accept resizes in small steps via the macOS accessibility API, so the window will visibly settle into its target size over about a second instead of snapping. This is a Gecko engine limitation, not a SupaSidebar bug. See [Browser Integration](/features/browserIntegration) for details.

## Keyboard Shortcuts

Shortcuts specific to the sidebar panel. For all shortcuts, see [Shortcuts](/features/shortcuts).

| Shortcut | Action |
|----------|--------|
| `Cmd Shift Space` | Show or hide sidebar |
| `Cmd Ctrl F` | Bring sidebar to front |
| `Cmd Ctrl L` | Switch between left and right |
| `Cmd Ctrl P` | Pin or unpin from current space |
| `Esc` | Hide sidebar |

## Notes

Add a note to any saved link to capture context, instructions, or anything else you want surfaced when you find the link later.

- **From the sidebar** - Click the **Edit Note** button that appears on a link row on hover, or right-click and choose **Add note** / **Edit note**.
- **From the Command Panel** - With a result selected, press `Opt Return` to open the actions panel, then **Add note**.
- **Save and dismiss** - `Cmd Return` saves, `Escape` cancels.

Notes are indexed by search. Typing a word that appears in a note surfaces the saved link, with the matching text highlighted in the result.

## Tips

- Use the presets panel in **Preferences - Sidebar** for one-click configuration of sidebar behavior. Choose from Minimal, Bookmark Manager, Workspace Switcher, or Browser Companion.
- Set a custom color on a single link to flag your "currently working on" item without pinning it.
- The noise texture pairs well with the glass theme for a polished look.
- See [Sidebar Access](/features/sidebarAccess) for all the ways to open the sidebar - floating button, mouse triggers, and more.
